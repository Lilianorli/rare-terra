Nature solved the rare earth recovery problem. We are industrializing it.

RareTerra turns microbial rare earth biochemistry into a clean, precise alternative to hydrometallurgy. Our nature-inspired, engineered microbes selectively capture and separate lanthanides at ambient conditions, using dense arrays of chelators and transporters tuned to subtle differences in ionic radius. Rare earths are biologically separated and purified, delivering spec-grade cuts from ore, concentrates, e-waste, and swarf.

Rare earths need a new playbook. We’re building it.

Today’s rare-earth supply is monopolized, chemically intensive, and fragile—driving high costs, waste, and uncertainty for everything from data center construction to EV manufacture. RareTerra’s biology-first platform is built to flip that script.

How our technology disrupts the STATUS QUO

Bio-selective recovery, not massive solvent plants

Our microbes and ligands target specific rare-earth ions at ambient conditions, shrinking or replacing long solvent-extraction cascades and cutting reagents, wastewater, and energy use.

Feedstock-flexible by design

We unlock value from magnets, mine tailings, process scrap, and low-grade ores that are uneconomic with conventional flowsheets—turning “waste” into reliable supply.

Distributed, modular refining

Compact, closed-loop units co-located with manufacturers or mines reduce logistics, diversify supply away from single-point bottlenecks, and build resilience into the chain.
